# Code of Conduct

This project is open to people from any background, level of experience, and viewpoint. To keep it useful and pleasant for everyone, here's what we expect from contributors, reviewers, maintainers, and anyone interacting in project spaces (issues, PRs, discussions, code review comments).

## What we expect

- **Be kind.** Disagree on technical merit, not on people. Assume good intent — most of the time someone isn't being obtuse on purpose.
- **Be specific.** "This doesn't work" is not feedback. "When I do X with input Y, I get Z and I expected W" is.
- **Be patient with newcomers.** Everyone is new at something. The friendly answer to a basic question is "here's the answer + here's the doc that covers it," not "RTFM."
- **Stay on topic.** Issues are for bugs and feature work. Pull requests are for changes. Discussions are for open-ended questions. Political and off-topic conversations belong elsewhere.
- **Credit other people's work.** If your PR builds on someone else's discussion, link the discussion. If it borrows an approach from another project, link the source.

## What's not acceptable

- Personal attacks, harassment, slurs, or targeted insults — in any project space, public or private.
- Doxxing, posting other people's private information, or repeatedly contacting someone who's asked you to stop.
- Sexualized language, imagery, or unwanted advances.
- Trolling, deliberately misrepresenting people, or arguing in bad faith.
- Retaliation against anyone who reports a concern.

These rules apply equally to maintainers and contributors. Status doesn't grant cover for unkind behavior.

## Reporting

If you see something that violates this, please report it privately. Don't pile on in-thread — it tends to make the situation worse.

Use one of these channels:

- The repo's GitHub **Security** tab → **Report a vulnerability** also works as a private channel for conduct reports. Mark the report as conduct-related in the subject line.
- Direct message to the maintainer listed in `package.json`'s `author` field.

When you report, please include:

- What happened (one or two sentences).
- Where it happened (issue number, PR number, discussion link).
- Who was involved (usernames).
- Whether you're willing to be contacted for follow-up.

You can report anonymously, but we may not be able to follow up with you for clarification.

## What happens after a report

- Acknowledgement within 3 business days.
- A maintainer reviews the report and any context (linked threads, prior reports).
- We may reach out to anyone involved for their account.
- We decide on a response and tell the reporter what we did.

Possible responses, roughly in order of severity:

- A private conversation asking for a change in behavior.
- A public correction in the thread.
- A temporary cooling-off period (no posting in project spaces for N days/weeks).
- Removal from the project.

For severe violations (harassment, doxxing, slurs) we skip straight to the bottom of the list.

We will not retaliate against good-faith reporters, even if the report doesn't result in action against the person reported.

## Maintainer accountability

Maintainers are held to the same standards. If you have a concern about a maintainer's conduct, you can report it through the channels above; reports about maintainers go to the other maintainer(s) for review. If there's only one maintainer and the report is about them, escalate via direct message to a project contributor whose judgment you trust, and they'll help mediate.
